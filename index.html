The Auspicious One

Lord Shiva, also known as Mahadeva, is one of the principal deities of Hinduism.

About Lord Shiva

Lord Shiva is known as "The Destroyer" within the Trimurti, the Hindu trinity that includes Brahma and Vishnu. He is regarded as limitless, transcendent, unchanging, formless, and the greatest Yogi who meditates for the well-being of the universe.

Legends and Mythology

Shiva in Meditation

Shiva is often depicted in deep meditation, symbolizing his inner peace and detachment from worldly matters.

Shiva Tandava

The cosmic dance of Shiva, known as Tandava, signifies the creation, preservation, and destruction of the universe.

Shiva and Parvati

Shiva's consort, Parvati, is the mother goddess and represents Shakti, the divine feminine energy.

Worship and Devotion

Lord Shiva is worshipped in various forms and aspects. The most common representation is the Shiva Linga, which symbolizes his infinite nature. Devotees observe fasts, chant mantras, and perform rituals to honor him, especially during the festivals of Maha Shivaratri and Shravan.
